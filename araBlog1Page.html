Journey in College

As a third-year BSIT student, reflecting on my college journey feels both nostalgic and empowering. The path I’ve taken hasn't been the easiest, but it’s been filled with experiences that shaped not only my knowledge but also my character. From the very first day of university to now, every project, late-night study session, and presentation has contributed to my growth in ways I never imagined.

I realized that I wasn't alone in this journey. I was fortunate to have friends by my side who were navigating the same challenges. Together, we found strength in our shared experiences—whether it was tackling a difficult assignment, discussing project ideas late into the night, or simply offering each other encouragement when the road got tough. Their camaraderie made the journey feel less isolating. Additionally, my family’s unwavering support became my anchor. Their belief in me, especially during moments when I questioned my own abilities, reminded me to stay grounded and focused on the bigger picture. Through group studies, shared laughter, and collective victories, we transformed difficult days into memorable moments of growth. Looking back, I now understand that the most significant lessons were not always learned in success, but in the struggles that challenged me to rise stronger, wiser, and more determined.

Looking back, I realize how far I’ve come—not just in terms of academics, but in confidence and maturity. College has been more than just learning from textbooks—it’s been about learning about myself. With one more year ahead, I feel hopeful, proud, and excited to keep going, knowing that every step taken is a step toward becoming a skilled IT professional.
