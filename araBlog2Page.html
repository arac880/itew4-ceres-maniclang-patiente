IT Experiences

Entering the IT field was never part of my original dream. I saw myself doing something different—but life had other plans. At first, the unfamiliar environment of programming, databases, and systems felt overwhelming. I struggled with imposter syndrome and wondered if I truly belonged in such a technical field. But with time, exposure, and persistence, things started to shift.

As I immersed myself in the world of IT, I discovered facets of it that resonated deeply with me—problem-solving, designing intuitive user interfaces, and the fulfilling experience of watching a project evolve from a simple idea into a fully functioning system. The challenges that once felt intimidating slowly became stepping stones for growth. Every error message, failed compilation, or unexpected bug taught me something new—not just about the code, but about patience, persistence, and critical thinking. Debugging, which once filled me with frustration, gradually turned into a puzzle I was eager to solve. I began to enjoy the structure and logic behind programming languages, the aesthetics of clean, user-friendly design, and the gratification that came with optimizing a system or solving a tricky issue. With each passing semester, I grew more confident in my abilities. Though the occasional self-doubt still lingers—as it often does in such a fast-moving and competitive field—I now meet it with a stronger sense of purpose and curiosity. The IT world is not just a career path for me anymore; it has become a space where I can continuously learn, create, and contribute in meaningful ways.

I may not have chosen IT as my dream at first, but now, I see why I was led here. Fate works in mysterious ways, and perhaps this field has more to offer me than I once believed. I’ve learned that it’s not always about starting with a dream—sometimes it’s about building one along the way. And right now, I’m exactly where I need to be.
